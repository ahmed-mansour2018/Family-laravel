Emily Tusk Obituary

Mrs. Emily Tusk

Mrs. Emily Tusk, one of Sheldon's most remarkable women passed away at the home of her son Albert Smith at Wales Center Sunday afternoon at 2 o'clock, March 3rd after a week's illness of influenza followed by pneumonia. Mrs. Tusk who was in her 96th year was born in France June 1833. Her maiden name was Pochel and as Emily Pochel she emigrated to America and settled with friends in the town of Sheldon where she spent nearly her entire life. An attractive vivacious personality combined with a keen intellect and amazing memory for historical facts which she retained to the end she is said to have been a living history in herself. In her early girlhood she was united in marriage to John Smith of Sheldon who later became a well known hotel man, owning Hotels at what was then known as the Bee Hive at Bennington, at Lancaster and Sheldon Center. Some years after the death of her husband she was again married to Dominick Tusk also of Sheldon whose death ocurred some time ago. Besides the son Albert with whom she had lived the past two years she is survived by five other sons Fred of Byron, Augustus of Williamson, Michigan, Edward of Lansing, Michigan, John and Frank Smith of Sheldon also two daughters, Mrs. George Nassoly of Niagara Falls and Mrs. Helen Suttel of Strykersville, A son Joseph Smith died at the age of 21. Also surviving are several grand children.

Funeral services were held from St. Cecilia's church, Sheldon Wednesday morning at 10 o'clock the Rev. Joseph A. Weisbecker officiating. Burial in St. Cecilia's cemetary.
